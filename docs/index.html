Opinions

        My identity as a woman conflicts with my idenity as a person in a world where women are not people but I wear it anyway. To be clear, I am only a woman for the glory and the profit, for the discounts and the handouts, for the love of the game, & to understand this painting.
         This work is titled "Ophelia," in reference to the Hamlet character, it's old, painted by Henrietta Rae in 1890. Rae did an incredible job depicting Ophelia as her own entity, seperate from Hamlet. Her story was a tragedy in its own right, Hamlet was her Claudius, but she did not play his Hamlet. But if you were to look up "Ophelia Painting" the search results would show you Millais' "Ophelia" which was incredibly influential and is to this day far more famous than the work I included here, which is tragic because Millais' version is worse. He depicted the aftermath of Ophelia's suicide. Painting her corpse weak, helpless, and beautiful without even depicting Hamlet her story is still about Hamlet. Both of these paintings are named "Ophelia" but one was named for the woman and the other for the plot device.

"Speaking thus is not merely speaking, but acting, in that I turn to the imagination, which is the generator of an actuality, so I am the sustainer or destroyer." - I said this in regards to speech acts independently of Hegel, coincidence is a wild thing.
         I am very fond of the idea that words can create reality, obviously it is important to distill everything from me to you & you to me. Even if I say something you won’t truly know what I’m thinking. I can only give you the approximation of my thoughts, and the same is true vice-versa, I think art is also this way. Where no matter what we have this aloneness that’s driven by the inherent isolation of consciousness, but I don’t think it has to be bad I think it just reflects the beauty of how we are always trying to achieve this impossible goal, and we do good enough to connect, to bridge our experiences as far as we can, but I think it means that sometimes we try to adapt to the way we think other people might understand. Art can sometimes get caught here, called disingenuous, but I don't look at art that way, I see art as communication before I see it as anything else.
